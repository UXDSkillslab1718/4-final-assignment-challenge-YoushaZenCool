Education

The User Experience (UX) Design course explores two fundamental questions: 

  1. How does a user experience a digital product or service?
  2. How can we design the best possible experience for them while meeting wider business objectives?

 

My user experience design skills

With a focus on design led by the psychology of user-needs, data gathering and analysis, the course taught me how to apply principles of cognitive science and computer programming to develop innovative and effective design solutions.

I discovered how UX designers gathered data through ethnographic research and combine these insights with other data sources such as website statistics and industry trends. I learned how to analyse and apply that data to devise and refine a digital experience that works best in any given situation.

My user experience design future

Throughout my degree my learning oriented around the application of user experience design principles in support of successful business models. I used industry standard techniques to develop professional solutions for a range of contexts; in each case moving from research to the creation of user journeys to the design of user experience design that works across multiple digital environments.

I learned how to work with the commercial sector through contact with businesses via live briefs and a student-led consultancy, developing project management, communication and collaborative working skills that will make your CV stand out to employers.
