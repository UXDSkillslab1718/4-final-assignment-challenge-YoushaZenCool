About Me

From Past to Present

My life begins on a cold Thursday night in the middle of January in the year 1998, in a small town called Rozenburg, I was born in a small apartment of my parents. Rozenburg was a small town with a small population, lots of nature, and lots of canals and dams. I remember nothing from that place, because we moved to Rotterdam when I was two years old. Specifically the south of Rotterdam, would be the place where I would grow up. But let's skip to the good part, middle school.

When I now look back on my middle school experience I really miss it, even though it was hard work all day, every day. Middle school would be the place for me where I learned about science and the beauty that came with it. The first 3 years of school, the classes we had we’re regulated by the school, but at the end of the third year we could choose our own classes. I choose predominately the scientific classes. I had physics, chemistry, biology, math type B and math type D. The subjects weren’t easy, the test were a pain in the butt, however the feeling of finally understanding it and passing the test is indescribable. This part of my life would also be where I discovered my hobbies and interests. One of my main hobbies is fitness. I started doing strength-training in the small gym of my school. If I had free time in between classes, you would find me there. I learned everything there was to learn about hypertrophy (the science behind growing muscle) and dieting. Eventually I got a gym subscription. An other hobby of mine that also started then, was making music. I have to thank my friend who introduced me to the technical part of editing tracks and recording. We would make some weird music, that didn’t sound good at first, but we kept working on it. I still do some light remixing in my free time.

After I graduated, I thought that a computer science bachelor would be the right pick for me. That turned not to be true. Although I share a great interest in the technical process of software, I felt out of place in that bachelor. Although the classes were interesting, the creativity and application of the theory was lacking. So I started looking for bachelors that combined the technical with the creative. After a long search I finally found the bachelor UXD. A term that I had vaguely heard before and new nothing more than only its name. I was intrigued, so I did some research on my own. I saw that UXD was connected to almost everything of the multi-media design process. I knew that this would ensure the possibility of working both creatively and technically. So, I decided to go to the open day and get a closer look. The open day confirmed my expectations and I finally enrolled myself.
